Quantum Bits (Qubits)

Unlike classical bits which can be either 0 or 1, qubits can exist in superposition, representing both 0 and 1 simultaneously.

Quantum Superposition

Superposition allows quantum systems to exist in multiple states simultaneously, exponentially increasing computational power.

Quantum Entanglement

Entanglement creates correlations between qubits, where the state of one qubit instantly influences another, regardless of distance.

Quantum Gates and Circuits

Quantum gates are the building blocks of quantum circuits, manipulating qubits through rotations and entanglement operations.

Hadamard (H) Gate: Creates a superposition state from |0⟩ or |1⟩ states, essential for quantum algorithms.
